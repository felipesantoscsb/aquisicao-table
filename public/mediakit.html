The Fit

Why This Works for a New York Hotel

I

New York is her genuine story, not a campaign stop

Evelyn lived six months in New York and calls it her favorite city in the world, on record. Hotel content slots into an affection her audience already knows - it reads as a homecoming, not an ad.

II

Her verticals are a hotel's verticals

Gastronomy, travel and quiet reflection - rooms, restaurants, rooftops and neighborhoods are the natural set of her content. She turns a property into a narrative arc: morning coffee, golden-hour streets, cinematic nights.

III

A high-value inbound audience

Her audience is Brazilian - consistently one of New York City's largest and highest-spending international visitor markets. She travels as a couple, matching the date-night and romantic-getaway packages NYC hotels sell best.

IV

Discovery reach, and assets you keep

With 73.6% of views from non-followers, a collaboration introduces the property to new travelers rather than re-showing it to fans. Reels, Stories, TikTok and stills can include usage rights for the hotel's own channels.
